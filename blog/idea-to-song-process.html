The 7-Step EchoGifts Creation Process

1

Share Your Story

The foundation of your personalized song

Everything starts with your story. Through our simple order form, you'll share the details that matter most—the emotions, memories, relationships, and moments you want captured in music. This isn't just basic information; it's the heart and soul of your future song.

What We Ask For:

  • The main story or theme: What's the central message or memory?
  • Key people involved: Who are the main characters in your story?
  • Emotional tone: Should it be celebratory, romantic, nostalgic, or uplifting?
  • Important details: Specific moments, places, or qualities that make it unique
  • Genre preference: What musical style best fits your story?

The more specific and personal you can be, the more authentic and meaningful your song will become.

2

AI Story Analysis

Advanced emotion and theme recognition

Once we receive your story, our advanced AI system begins the analysis process. This isn't simple keyword matching—our technology understands context, emotion, and narrative structure to identify the musical elements that will best represent your story.

What The AI Identifies:

  • Emotional arc: How feelings change throughout your story
  • Key themes: Love, triumph, loss, celebration, growth, family bonds
  • Narrative structure: Beginning, middle, end, and emotional peaks
  • Relationship dynamics: How people connect and interact in your story
  • Temporal elements: Past memories, present moments, future hopes

This analysis ensures every musical choice—from tempo to key signature—serves your story's emotional journey.

3

Musical Composition Creation

Original melody, harmony, and rhythm generation

With your story's emotional blueprint in place, our AI composer creates original music that matches your narrative perfectly. Every note, chord, and rhythmic pattern is generated specifically for your story—not pulled from existing songs.

Musical Elements We Create:

  • Melody: The main vocal line that carries your story's emotional message
  • Harmony: Supporting chords that add depth and emotional resonance
  • Rhythm: The beat and tempo that matches your story's energy level
  • Arrangement: Instrument selection and layering for your chosen genre
  • Structure: Verse-chorus format optimized for your narrative flow

Tips for the Best Results

Be Specific with Details

  • Include actual names, places, and dates that matter to your story
  • Describe specific moments rather than general feelings
  • Mention unique personality traits or quirks of the people involved
  • Share why certain memories are meaningful to you

Choose the Right Genre

Consider both the story's emotion and the recipient's music preferences. A romantic ballad works well for anniversary gifts, while uplifting pop might be perfect for graduation celebrations.

Think About the Listener

While your story is important, consider how the recipient will feel hearing it. Focus on positive memories and emotions that will bring them joy when they listen.
